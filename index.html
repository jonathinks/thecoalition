The Coalition

We are a group of young people spread around the world who have gathered around the idea that the current education system is inadequate for achieving a sustainable and peaceful world. We as individuals work on a wide range of projects that challenge the education system in different ways while sharing inspiration and finding synergies. The projects we work on are learner-centered education advocacy, open protocols for decentralized accreditation, language learning for cultural understanding and empathy, research in adaptive and personalized learning, and learner-centered migrant school curriculum design.

We don’t believe a single actor can, nor should, come with a silver-bullet solution. Creating the conditions for an educated and informed population is a collective effort with many actors coming together using their unique strengths and perspectives. As designers, engineers, entrepreneurs, researchers, software developers and teachers we contribute with different ideas and solutions. If you want to share your perspective or discuss a certain topic, please reach out to hello@jonathannylander.se.

Dana Gretton - Decentralized Accreditation for Education

Meeting #2, 2017-10-23

Dana Gretton is a recent MIT graduate in EECS who believes that quality universal education is a cornerstone of a sustainable global future. He is developing a decentralized system of accreditation to provide a trustworthy way for people to get credit for teaching other people, formally or informally, and implementing it in such a way that quality teachers get reliably accredited and poor teachers don't. He currently works at the MIT Media Lab as a laboratory technician for robotically automated biological experiments researching gene drives and directed evolution.
